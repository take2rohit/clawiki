# World Models — Overview

> A synthesis of 20 papers spanning the emergence, maturation, and scaling of world models in AI — from Ha & Schmidhuber's 2018 dream-training prototype to NVIDIA's billion-parameter Physical AI foundation models in 2025.

---

## What Are World Models and Why Do They Matter?

A world model is a learned internal representation of environment dynamics that allows an agent to simulate the consequences of its actions without executing them in the real world. The core appeal is sample efficiency: instead of learning a policy through millions of costly real interactions, an agent can train inside an imagined "dream" generated by the world model, using model-free RL algorithms on synthetic experience. The world model absorbs the complexity of the environment; the policy operates on compact, structured representations of its predictions.

At a deeper level, world models are hypothesized to be central to how biological intelligence works. LeCun's [[lecun-2022-openreview]] ([JEPA](papers/lecun-2022-openreview.md)) position paper argues that the key missing ingredient in current AI is not more data or larger networks, but an internal model of the world that allows prediction, planning, and counterfactual reasoning — the kind of common-sense understanding that allows a human teenager to learn to drive in 20 hours rather than 20 million miles.

## The Major Paradigms

### 1. Latent Dynamics for RL (Dreamer / TD-MPC lineage)

The dominant paradigm in model-based RL learns a compact latent-space transition model and uses it to train policies through imagination, never decoding back to pixels during policy updates. [[ha-2018-neurips]] ([World Models](papers/ha-2018-neurips.md)) established the template: a VAE encodes frames to latent z; an MDN-RNN models temporal dynamics; a tiny controller is optimized by CMA-ES entirely inside hallucinated rollouts. This V/M/C decomposition — Vision, Memory, Controller — is inherited by every subsequent Dreamer paper.

Hafner et al. refined this into the Recurrent State-Space Model (RSSM) in [[hafner-2019-icml]] ([PlaNet](papers/hafner-2019-icml.md)), added an actor-critic trained in latent imagination in [[hafner-2021-iclr]] ([DreamerV2](papers/hafner-2021-iclr.md)), and in [[hafner-2023-arxiv]] ([DreamerV3](papers/hafner-2023-arxiv.md)) introduced a suite of robustness techniques — symlog transformations, twohot regression, percentile return normalization — that make a single set of hyperparameters work across 150+ tasks spanning Atari, Minecraft, DMControl, ProcGen, and more. DreamerV3 is the first algorithm to collect diamonds in Minecraft from scratch without human data.

In continuous control, [[hansen-2022-icml]] ([TD-MPC](papers/hansen-2022-icml.md)) and [[hansen-2024-iclr]] ([TD-MPC2](papers/hansen-2024-iclr.md)) pair latent dynamics with MPPI planning (rather than actor-critic imagination), achieving log-linear performance scaling from 1M to 317M parameters across 80 diverse tasks.

### 2. Video Generation as World Simulation

A parallel paradigm treats world modeling as video prediction: given past frames and a conditioning signal (text, trajectory, action), generate realistic future video. This is the approach taken by [[hu-2023-arxiv]] ([GAIA-1](papers/hu-2023-arxiv.md)) (Wayve) and [[agarwal-2025-arxiv]] ([Cosmos](papers/agarwal-2025-arxiv.md)) (NVIDIA) in autonomous driving, by [[bar-2024-cvpr]] ([Navigation World Models](papers/bar-2024-cvpr.md)) for navigation, and by [[alonso-2024-neurips]] ([DIAMOND](papers/alonso-2024-neurips.md)) for discrete-action RL. The world model operates in pixel space rather than a compact latent, which enables interpretable, playable simulations (DIAMOND demonstrates an interactive CS:GO neural game engine) but requires managing compounding prediction errors. DIAMOND's key finding is that the EDM diffusion framework is dramatically more stable than DDPM for autoregressive rollouts — stable at 1,000 imagined steps with a single denoising step — yielding a new Atari 100k SOTA of 1.46 mean HNS.

### 3. JEPA and Predictive Representation Learning

[[lecun-2022-openreview]] ([JEPA](papers/lecun-2022-openreview.md)) argues against generative (pixel-space) world models entirely. Predicting future pixels forces the model to represent irrelevant stochastic details (leaf ripples, background textures) that are not causally relevant to the agent's goals. Instead, JEPA trains encoders and a predictor to predict in abstract representation space — the encoder is invariant to unpredictable details, keeping only the causally structured information. Hierarchical H-JEPA stacks multiple such levels to yield representations at progressively coarser time scales, enabling hierarchical planning. This paradigm influenced I-JEPA, V-JEPA, and V-JEPA 2 from Meta FAIR, and is increasingly influential in the robotics community.

### 4. Foundation Model and LLM-Backed World Models

The most recent development is treating world models as foundation models: large-scale pre-training on diverse video data, followed by domain-specific fine-tuning. [[agarwal-2025-arxiv]] ([Cosmos](papers/agarwal-2025-arxiv.md)) is the most developed example — 7B/14B diffusion and 4B/12B autoregressive transformers trained on 100M curated video clips from 20M hours of diverse footage, open-weight, with post-training recipes for driving, manipulation, and camera control. [[mazzaglia-2024-neurips]] ([GenRL](papers/mazzaglia-2024-neurips.md)) takes a complementary approach: bridge a generative RL world model with a pretrained VLM (InternVideo2) via a small connector-aligner module, enabling data-free policy learning from language or video prompts in 30 minutes without language annotations.

## The Trajectory: 2018 → DreamerV3 → Foundation Models

The arc from [[ha-2018-neurips]] ([World Models](papers/ha-2018-neurips.md)) to the present is one of progressive unification and scaling. Ha & Schmidhuber showed that a world model can replace the real environment for policy training — but only on two simple tasks. The Dreamer line (PlaNet, DreamerV2, DreamerV3) systematically removed domain-specific tuning requirements while extending to increasingly diverse and complex environments. DreamerV3 represents a kind of plateau for the latent-dynamics paradigm: a single agent that performs well across virtually every standard RL benchmark, demonstrating that the core architectural choices (RSSM, categorical latents, reconstruction-based SSL) are robust and general.

The next phase is scaling: not training world models per task or domain, but pre-training large world models on massive diverse video data and fine-tuning for specific applications. This mirrors the LLM paradigm and is most explicitly realized in [[agarwal-2025-arxiv]] ([Cosmos](papers/agarwal-2025-arxiv.md)). The survey by [[ding-2024-csur]] ([Ding et al.](papers/ding-2024-csur.md)) maps this entire landscape — from MBRL implicit world models through Sora-class video generators to social simulacra — under a unified taxonomy of "understanding vs. prediction."

## Key Open Questions

**Evaluation gap.** Standard metrics (next-token accuracy, task success, FID/FVD) are structurally insensitive to world model quality. [[vafa-2024-neurips]] ([Evaluating WM](papers/vafa-2024-neurips.md)) demonstrates this sharply: a transformer with 1.00 next-token test accuracy on NYC routing has sequence compression precision of 0.10; GPT-4 achieves perfect logic-puzzle task accuracy with compression precision 0.21. The Myhill-Nerode framework provides theoretically grounded behavioral metrics (compression and distinction) but requires knowing the ground-truth world state space, limiting its current applicability to formal domains.

**Generalization and cross-task transfer.** DreamerV3 uses fixed hyperparameters but trains from scratch per task. TD-MPC2's few-shot experiments show 2× improvement from pre-training, but this is not yet transfer in the foundation model sense. True zero-shot generalization of world model dynamics to new physics, morphologies, or scene types remains elusive.

**3D understanding and spatial grounding.** Most world models operate in 2D observation space (pixels or proprioceptive state vectors). [[kong-2025-arxiv]] ([3D/4D Survey](papers/kong-2025-arxiv.md)) argues that genuine world understanding requires 3D structure and 4D (spatio-temporal) dynamics — capabilities that current video generators approximate but do not reliably achieve. Geometric consistency, depth, occlusion, and object permanence systematically fail in current large video models.

**Physics and causality.** [[ding-2024-csur]] ([Ding et al.](papers/ding-2024-csur.md)) documents that even Sora-class models fail basic physics tests (gravity, fluid dynamics, thermal processes). The root cause is distributional: data-driven scaling cannot recover causal physical laws from correlational video data. Hybrid approaches embedding physics priors or PDE constraints are nascent but promising.

**Scaling laws.** TD-MPC2 shows log-linear performance scaling in continuous control; DreamerV3 shows monotonic improvement from 12M to 400M parameters. But systematic scaling laws for world model quality — analogous to Chinchilla laws for LLMs — have not been derived. Whether world model capabilities (causal reasoning, counterfactual accuracy) scale predictably with data and compute is an open empirical question.

## How the 20 Papers in This Corpus Relate

The corpus divides into three groups. **Seminal foundations** (5 papers): [[ha-2018-neurips]] ([World Models](papers/ha-2018-neurips.md)), [[hafner-2019-icml]] ([PlaNet](papers/hafner-2019-icml.md)), [[hafner-2021-iclr]] ([DreamerV2](papers/hafner-2021-iclr.md)), [[hafner-2023-arxiv]] ([DreamerV3](papers/hafner-2023-arxiv.md)), [[lecun-2022-openreview]] ([JEPA](papers/lecun-2022-openreview.md)) — these establish the core architectures and conceptual frameworks that all other papers build on or react to.

**Methodological advances** (8 papers): [[micheli-2023-iclr]] ([IRIS](papers/micheli-2023-iclr.md)), [[alonso-2024-neurips]] ([DIAMOND](papers/alonso-2024-neurips.md)), [[robine-2023-iclr]] ([TWM](papers/robine-2023-iclr.md)), [[hansen-2022-icml]] ([TD-MPC](papers/hansen-2022-icml.md)), [[hansen-2024-iclr]] ([TD-MPC2](papers/hansen-2024-iclr.md)), [[mazzaglia-2024-neurips]] ([GenRL](papers/mazzaglia-2024-neurips.md)), [[hu-2023-arxiv]] ([GAIA-1](papers/hu-2023-arxiv.md)), [[bar-2024-cvpr]] ([Navigation WM](papers/bar-2024-cvpr.md)), [[min-2024-cvpr]] ([DriveWorld](papers/min-2024-cvpr.md)), [[agarwal-2025-arxiv]] ([Cosmos](papers/agarwal-2025-arxiv.md)) — each pushes the frontier in a specific direction (transformer dynamics, diffusion world models, continuous control scaling, VLM grounding, driving simulation, foundation scaling).

**Surveys and meta-analysis** (4 papers): [[ding-2024-csur]] ([Comprehensive Survey](papers/ding-2024-csur.md)), [[feng-2025-arxiv]] ([AD Survey](papers/feng-2025-arxiv.md)), [[li-2025-arxiv]] ([Embodied AI Survey](papers/li-2025-arxiv.md)), [[kong-2025-arxiv]] ([3D/4D Survey](papers/kong-2025-arxiv.md)) — provide the taxonomic frameworks for understanding the landscape; [[vafa-2024-neurips]] ([Evaluating WM](papers/vafa-2024-neurips.md)) uniquely addresses the meta-question of whether any of these systems are genuine world models at all.

The common thread is that every paper in this corpus, from different angles, grapples with the same fundamental question: how does a system learn to model the dynamics of a world from data, and what does it mean for that model to be correct?
